## Three things you can only experience in Chengdu

**1. Giant pandas, up close, in their real habitat.** Chengdu is the panda capital of the world. We go early, when they're active, and arrange a translated talk with conservation researchers.

**2. The Sanxingdui mystery.** A 3,000-year-old civilization that cast bronze masks with protruding eyes and three-meter-tall figures — then vanished without a trace. It looks like nothing else in human history. Archaeologists still can't agree on who they were.

**3. China's slow life.** Chengdu is famous across China for *not* rushing. Tea houses, park life, three-hour dinners. After a week in Beijing or Shanghai's intensity, Chengdu is the exhale.

### The ancient engineering you didn't expect

On day 5, you'll walk through Dujiangyan — an irrigation system built in 256 BC that **still works today**, feeding 5,000 square kilometers of farmland. No dam, no pumps, just brilliant ancient engineering.
