## Where China was born

Xi'an is the deepest of deep history. For over a thousand years it was China's capital — through the Qin (who unified the country), the Han, and the Tang (China's golden age). The Silk Road started here. The Terracotta Army was buried here.

If Beijing is modern China's political center and Shanghai its business center, **Xi'an is where the civilization itself began.**

### The three things you'll never forget

- **The Terracotta Army** — 8,000 soldiers, each with a unique face, buried for 2,200 years. Nothing prepares you for the scale.
- **Biking the City Wall** at sunset — 14 kilometers of perfect 14th-century fortification, the old city glowing inside, the new city outside.
- **The Muslim Quarter** at night — a street food scene that exists because Arab and Persian merchants settled here along the Silk Road, 1,300 years ago.

### The layer you don't expect

Beneath the ancient surface, modern Xi'an is one of China's aerospace hubs. For travelers curious about both layers, this is a city where the past and the future genuinely share the same streets.
